Best practices for engagement-driven messaging?

I've been trying out different messaging strategies to boost engagement rates with subscribers. Has anyone found a good balance between personal messages and promotional content?

So far, I've noticed that personal check-ins get more responses, but promotional messages about new content drive more immediate sales. I'm curious if anyone has found an optimal schedule or approach that balances both effectively.

Also, has anyone experimented with segmenting subscribers based on their engagement levels? Any tools or techniques you'd recommend?

Marketing Engagement Messaging

24 Replies

JakeCreates
Expert
2 hours ago

I've had success with a 70/30 approach - 70% personal engagement and 30% promotional. The key is making even your promotional messages feel personalized and valuable rather than just "buy this."

For segmentation, I use the built-in tools to create groups based on subscription length and purchase history. Long-term subscribers get more exclusive/early access messages, while newer ones get more "getting to know you" content.

LuciaStars
Elite
1 hour ago

I schedule my messages strategically throughout the week. Monday/Thursday for personal check-ins, Wednesday for a content preview, and weekend for main promotional messages.

Also, try using polls and questions in your messages - they dramatically increase engagement rates and give you valuable feedback about what your subscribers want to see.

Sophia
Expert
45 minutes ago

I've been testing a tiered approach based on subscriber activity. For my most engaged subscribers, I focus on exclusivity and behind-the-scenes content. For less active ones, I use more direct check-ins and special offers to re-engage them.

One thing that's worked well is sending voice messages occasionally instead of text. They feel much more personal and get great response rates.
